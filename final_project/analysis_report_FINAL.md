# Energy Demand & Temperature Extremes: Risk Analysis

**Data:** 43,361 hourly observations from AEP (2012-2017)

**Region:** Midwest US (Chicago, Detroit, Indianapolis, Pittsburgh average temperature)

## Key Findings

1. **Mean demand:**  14920  MW (SD =  2501  MW)
2. **Peak demand:**  24739  MW
3. **Temperature range:**  -25.2 °C to  34.1 °C

## Extreme Value Analysis

### Return Levels (GEV - Block Maxima)

- ** 1 -year:**  23441  MW
- ** 2 -year:**  23963  MW
- ** 5 -year:**  24519  MW
- ** 10 -year:**  24858  MW

### Return Levels (GPD - POT)

- ** 1 -year:**  25458  MW
- ** 2 -year:**  25537  MW
- ** 5 -year:**  25625  MW
- ** 10 -year:**  25682  MW

## Risk Metrics

- ** VaR(95%) :**  19398  MW
- ** VaR(99%) :**  21174  MW
- ** ES(99%) :**  22019  MW
- ** 10-Year Return Level :**  25682  MW

## Interpretation

The 10-year return level indicates that demand exceeding ** 25682  MW** is expected approximately once every 10 years.  Both GEV and GPD methods yield consistent results, confirming the robustness of our estimates.

The U-shaped demand-temperature relationship confirms that extreme temperatures (both hot and cold)  drive increased electricity demand due to heating and cooling needs.
